The Gueuze Project

Grand finale · Oct 14, 2028

A gueuze is a blend of young and aged spontaneously-fermented lambics, refermented in the bottle. Three vintages are aging simultaneously — the 2025, 2026 "faux" (kettle-soured), and 2026 "real" (open fermentation) lambics. They will be blended in January 2028 and released on October 14, 2028 — the brewery's tenth anniversary — as the grand finale of the BJCP quest.

Follow the project in the weekly newsletter, where every lambic brew day, every gravity reading, and every tasting note from the aging barrels has been documented across more than 45 issues.

Belgium · Germany · Czechia

July 2025 · 18 days · 11 cities · with Herb

Three weeks, three countries, 74 beers, ~19 styles. From the lambic bars of Brussels and the hidden alleys of Bruges to the smoky taverns of Bamberg, the tank Pilsners of Prague, and the Biergärten of Munich. A deep dive into the heartland of European brewing tradition.

Brussels July 6–11

Home base for the Belgian leg. Café Delirium Village (2,004 beers on offer), a rooftop bar with views of the old city, and the unmissable Cantillon brewery — the living cathedral of spontaneous fermentation. A pivotal Gueuze at Moeder Lambic.

Tripel Karmeliet Leffe Blond Cantillon Gueuze Lambic Kriek

Bruges July 8

A canal-laced medieval city with one goal: De Garre, hidden down an alley barely a meter wide. First in the door at noon for the dangerously smooth Tripel van de Garre (11.5% ABV) — served with a small cheese plate as a matter of policy.

Tripel van de Garre 11.5% Straffe Hendrik

Ghent July 9

A day trip to De Dulle Griet for their Max van 't Huis beer, served in a Kwak glass — the bulb-bottomed coachman's vessel designed in the 1790s so drivers could drink without spilling. The bar was closed on arrival; worth waiting for.

Max van 't Huis Kwak glass

Mechelen July 10

Het Anker Brewery, with records going back to the 15th century and five generations of the van Breedam family. Home of the Gouden Carolus (Golden Charles) line of strong Belgian ales, named for the gold coins of Emperor Charles V.

Gouden Carolus Classic Gouden Carolus Whisky Infused Belgian Dark Strong

Düsseldorf July 12

The spiritual home of Altbier — the "old beer" made with ale yeast fermented cold, producing something between a lager and an ale. The Altstadt brewpubs serve it in small 0.2L Stange glasses, perpetually refilled by roving waiters until you cover your glass.

Altbier Uerige Alt Füchschen Alt

Köln / Cologne July 13

A rest day in the city of Kölsch — the crisp, delicate ale that may only legally bear that name if brewed within the city limits. The Cologne Cathedral, Roman walls embedded in the sidewalk, and the brewery taprooms of the Altstadt. One beer, done perfectly.

Kölsch Früh Kölsch Reissdorf

Nuremberg July 14

Imperial city of the Holy Roman Empire, UNESCO World Heritage site, and home to a rich brewing tradition. Medieval gates, a moated old city, and the shadow of the postwar trials — history runs very deep here. Franconian lagers and the local brewing culture explored.

Franconian Lager Kellerbier

Bamberg July 15

The undisputed capital of Rauchbier. Three breweries in one day: Fässla, Schlenkerla (the mother lode of smoke beer), and Spezial — all recommended by neighbor Tobias, a German native who knows these streets. A direct comparison to the Rauchbier brewed at home.

Schlenkerla Rauchbier Märzen Schlenkerla Urbock Spezial Rauchbier Fässla Lagerbier

Plzeň July 16

The city that invented the pale lager. A tour of the Pilsner Urquell brewery and the story of how citizens revolted against bad beer in 1838, hired a Bavarian brewer, and accidentally changed the course of brewing history. The world's first golden lager, tasted at its source.

Pilsner Urquell Czech Pale Lager Unfiltered Pilsner

Prague July 17–18

In pursuit of "tank beer" — unfiltered Pilsner Urquell served directly from conditioning tanks, unpasteurized and alive. A city of extraordinary beauty, extraordinary beer culture, and the discovery that not all tank beer is created equal. U Fleků, a 500-year-old brewpub, for dark lager.

Tank Pilsner Urquell U Fleků Dark Lager Czech Dark Lager

Munich July 19–22

The grand finale. Three days, 8.2 liters (Peter's share). The Paulaner Biergarten, the Hofbräuhaus, the English Garden. Missing a train connection at Schwandorf turned into an unexpected beer discovery at a tiny Bahnhof market. Semper Gumby.

Michigan · Wisconsin · Minnesota

June 2024 · 4 days · 7 breweries · solo road trip

A solo road trip through the American Midwest, bookended by a visit to Josh Emrick — former student, dear friend, and the person responsible for Peter's homebrewing career — in Ann Arbor, and capped by a week of choral singing with Mastersingers USA in Minneapolis. Eight breweries across three states, including a family-farm-turned-brewery in rural Wisconsin and a chemistry professor's taproom in Appleton.

Grand Rapids, MI June 10

Three breweries in one day. Founders Brewing — one of the defining craft breweries of the Midwest, home of KBS and CBS. City Built Brewing, whose taproom reflects the diversity of the city. Brass Ring Brewing to close out the evening.

Founders KBS Founders Centennial IPA City Built Brass Ring

Battle Creek, MI June 11

New Holland Brewing's Battle Creek outpost — visited on the strength of Dragon's Milk, their bourbon-barrel porter widely available in California. The Battle Creek location is a brewpub only; the beer itself is made in Holland, MI on the shores of Lake Michigan.

Dragon's Milk Barrel-Aged Porter
Read the full account →
Kalamazoo, MI June 11

Bell's Eccentric Café — the taproom of Bell's Brewery, one of the oldest and most influential craft breweries in the Midwest. Home of Two Hearted Ale, perennially among the most celebrated American IPAs. A pilgrimage stop for any serious beer traveler in Michigan.

Bell's Two Hearted Ale Bell's Oberon Bell's Kalamazoo Stout

Elkhorn, WI June 12

Duesterbeck's Brewing — a sixth-generation family farm in rural Wisconsin that pivoted from dairy and pigs to craft beer in the 1980s. Arrived an hour early due to a forgotten time zone change. Owner Laura came out to the parking lot and invited him to sit in the shade until they opened. A very Wisconsin welcome.

Farm Brewery Craft Lager

Appleton, WI June 12

McFleshman's Brewing — founded by Allison Fleshman, Associate Professor of Chemistry at Lawrence University, and her husband Bob, on their sixth anniversary of opening. A private tour arranged by historian colleague Brigid Vance. The brewery's dream dinner guests: Hildegard von Bingen, who first added hops to beer in the 12th century.

McFleshman's House Beers Homebrewer-Founded

Minneapolis, MN June 13

The Yard House in St. Louis Park — a suburb that counts the Coen Brothers, Al Franken, Thomas Friedman, and the real Charlie Brown among its notable alumni. The Minneapolis leg capped the beertinerary before a week of choral singing with Mastersingers USA. A COVID-19 infection was the parting gift.

Massachusetts · New York

May 2024 · 9 days · 13 breweries · solo road trip

Sandwiched between a choral workshop at Tanglewood with Berkshire Choral International and a 55th reunion at Amherst College, a solo road trip through western Massachusetts and upstate New York. Thirteen breweries in nine days — from a Baltic Porter specialist in Framingham to a Belgian ale destination in Cooperstown, and ending at Treehouse in South Deerfield where a 50/50 perfect beer was found.

Framingham, MA May 22

Jack's Abby Craft Lagers — specialists in lager fermented with ale character. Home of Framinghammer Baltic Porter (10% ABV), introduced by brother Manny and beloved ever since. A name that references the city, the effect of overconsumption, and an actual tool — all at once.

Framinghammer Baltic Porter Craft Lagers

Lenox & Lee, MA May 22–26

Antimony Brewing — founded by former chemical engineer Rob Trask, whose periodic-table obsession runs through every element of the brewery. Antimony is element #51; his Lee outpost is called 51 Park. The double wolf-head logo references alchemy. Visited four times over five days while singing at Tanglewood.

Dalton, MA May 23

Shire Beer Co. — a Berkshires stopover on the way between Tanglewood commitments. Small, local, and worth the detour for anyone making their way through western Massachusetts.
